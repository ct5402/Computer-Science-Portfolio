Hardware

The motherboard is made up of a circut board that is made out of a rigid plastic. The circuits are usually made of a thin layer of ethier copper or aluminum. It is usually located at the bottom of the computer case. The motherboard is what routes everything.

Parts of a Motherboard

CPU

RAM

RAM stands for Random Acess Memory. What it does is it stores temporary things. When you shut your computer off the information will be gone. Data is stored randomly across the board rather then in sequence. It can be transferred at random to the processer. Stores all the processes programs use that memory to run.

Hard Drive

The hard drive is what stores everything on your computer. Your OS, files, doccuments, photos, prefrences and more. If your hard drive were to be damaged everything on it would be gone. Everything on the hard drive is measured in terms of size like megabytes, gigabytes, and terabytes. There are two different types of hard drives "HDD" which is a spinning hard drive that uses a mechanical arm to spin the disk and "SSD" has no moving parts in it.

CPU stands for "Central Processing Unit". The CPU is very important because it is what processes the information and commands. It basically handles all the instructions you give to your computer. Everything goes through the CPU. Even simple actions like pressing keys on the keyboard go through the CPU.



Monitor, Keyboard, Mouse

Monitor:

The monitor is made up of small LED lights that work together to create the color on the screen. The monitor is connected to the system unit which is where the computer parts are.

Keyboard:

The keyboard is the keys with letters. It is used to type. It takes physical input and transforms it to a digital output. There are different types of keyboards with different switches like the rubber dom, mechanical cherry switch, and topre.

Mouse:

The mouse is what moves the pointer around on the monitor. The OS recognizes the mouse and is constantly listening for input from the mouse
